# Philatelic Terminology

- **Definitive**: Stamps intended for regular mail over a long period.
- **Commemorative**: Stamps issued for a specific event or anniversary.
- **Overprint**: Additional printing on a stamp after it was originally produced (e.g., for local use).
- **Surcharge**: An overprint that changes the face value of the stamp.
- **Imperforate**: Stamps produced without perforations, intended to be cut apart.
- **Error**: A mistake in production (e.g., inverted center, wrong color).
- **Plate Block**: A block of stamps with the plate serial number in the margin.
- **First Day Cover (FDC)**: An envelope with a stamp cancelled on its first day of issue.
- **Watermark**: A pattern in the paper visible when held to light or placed in fluid.
- **Perforation Gauge**: A tool used to measure the number of perforations in 2 centimeters.
