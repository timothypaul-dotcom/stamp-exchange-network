# Buying and Exchange Guide

## Identifying a Stamp
1. **Country**: Usually printed on the stamp (sometimes in native language/script).
2. **Denomination**: The face value.
3. **Subject**: The image or text depicted.
4. **Perforations**: Use a gauge to check measurements.
5. **Watermark**: Check for specific paper markings.

## Verification Checklist
- **Check for Repairs**: Look for filled holes, re-gumming, or reperforation under UV light or magnification.
- **Expertization**: For high-value stamps, look for certificates (e.g., Philatelic Foundation, APS).
- **Pricing**: Consult catalogs like Scott (US), Stanley Gibbons (UK), Michel (Germany), or Yvert et Tellier (France).

## Exchange Best Practices
- **Value Matching**: Use catalog values (CV) as a baseline, but consider market demand.
- **Condition Parity**: Ensure stamps being traded are of comparable grade unless the difference is balanced by quantity or rarity.
- **Safe Shipping**: Use glassine envelopes and stiff cardboard to prevent bending/moisture damage.
