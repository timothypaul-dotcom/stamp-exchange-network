# Stamp Grading Standards

## Centering
- **Superb (S)**: Perfectly centered with four large, even margins.
- **Extra Fine (XF)**: Nearly perfect centering, margins slightly uneven.
- **Very Fine (VF)**: Well-centered, margins clear of the design on all sides.
- **Fine (F)**: Design is clear of perforations but margins are noticeably uneven.
- **Average (VG/G)**: Perforations may touch or slightly cut into the design.

## Gum Condition (Unused Stamps)
- **Mint Never Hinged (MNH)**: Original gum is intact with no disturbance.
- **Mint Hinged (MH)**: Original gum with a small mark or remnant from a hinge.
- **Large Part Original Gum (LPOG)**: Significant amount of original gum remains.
- **Regummed**: New gum applied to simulate MNH; reduces value.

## Perforations
- **Intact**: All teeth are present and uniform.
- **Short Perf**: A perforation tooth is shorter than the others.
- **Pulled Perf**: A perforation tooth is missing entirely.
- **Reperforated**: Perforations manually added to an imperforate stamp or to "improve" centering.

## Cancellation (Used Stamps)
- **Lightly Cancelled**: Postmark is faint, design is clearly visible.
- **Circular Date Stamp (CDS)**: Clear, readable town and date.
- **Heavy/Bull's Eye**: Cancellation obscures much of the design.
- **Fancy Cancel**: Artistic or unusual postmarks (can increase value).
